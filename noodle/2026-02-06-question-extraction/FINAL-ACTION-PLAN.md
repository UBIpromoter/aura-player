## Summary

We reviewed all 411 active world questions, 210 assessment items, and 10 onboarding questions across ChatGPT, Grok, Gemini, Claude Opus, Claude Sonnet, and Claude Haiku. Two rounds produced universal consensus on the core problems and clear direction on fixes.

**The diagnosis:** Great raw content, bad pacing and balance. Too many "homework" questions diluting the fun ones. Judgment category at 55% creates opinion-polling fatigue. The first session experience isn't curated for hooks.

**The fix:** Archive ~45 low-energy questions, add ~30 high-energy new ones, curate the first-serve experience, and lean into Shadow Self as a differentiator.

## Action 4: Shadow Self — Lean In

### The Decision

Promote Shadow Self. Don't hide it, don't soften it. It's opt-in — curious people seek it out, others skip it. Self-awareness is the reward.

### Naming

**Test Name: Shadow Self**

Grok argued convincingly to keep it — it's evocative, memorable, and has strong brand energy. "Hidden Playbook" tested well with Gemini and Chat but feels softer than the content deserves. Shadow Self says what it is.

**Subscale Names:**

| Construct | Current | Final | Rationale |
|-----------|---------|-------|-----------|
| Machiavellianism | Strategic Mind | **Strategic Mind** | All reviewers approved. Clear, intriguing. |
| Narcissism | Self-Image | **Self-Image** | Works well. "Presence" and "Status Drive" were proposed but Self-Image is more honest. |
| Psychopathy | Detachment | **Cold Focus** | Chat's suggestion. "Detachment" felt flat/clinical. "Cold Focus" sounds like a strength while being accurate. |

### Intro Text

> "Most people have a public self and a private playbook. This explores how you handle power, attention, and empathy when there's no social reward for being nice."

### Results Framing

- **Tone:** Curious observer, not therapist or moral arbiter. Self-knowledge as power.
- **Structure per scale:** Percentile context + insight + balanced framing
- **No good/bad language.** Instead: "Upside when controlled" and "Risk when unchecked"
- **Example high Strategic Mind:** "You naturally think several moves ahead. People with similar patterns often excel in negotiation, leadership, or creative fields where reading situations deeply pays off. Watch for: sometimes connection matters more than winning."
- **Example low Strategic Mind:** "Authenticity is your default mode. People trust you implicitly. Watch for: not everyone plays by the same rules you do."

### Unlock Positioning

Tease earlier in the app flow. Don't gate behind full Tier 2 completion. Consider:
- A teaser after Quick Profile: "Want to know your hidden playbook?"
- Or unlock after any 2-3 Starter Pack modules instead of all 5

## Action 5: System Improvements (Queue for Implementation)

These aren't content changes — they're algorithm and UX work. Prioritized by reviewer urgency.

### Priority: High (Bump Up)

**1. Confidence Slider Surfacing**
Multiple reviewers said don't delay this. Even a simple post-session insight adds immediate delight:
- "Your Hot Takes style: Firm on social questions, Hunchy on predictions"
- "You're more certain than 78% of users"
- "Certain" answers on predictions → tagged as "Hot Takes" in profile

**2. Serving Algorithm Pacing**
Enforce before the full algorithm ships:
- Temperature lanes: Light → Medium → Light → Deep → Light
- Hard rule: never two heavy questions in a row
- Category rotation: never 3+ Judgment in a row
- Philosophy spacing: max 2 per 10-question window

**3. Prediction Expiry System**
Add a `date_expiry` field. Flag questions within 3 months of their date. Review quarterly. Dead predictions make the app look abandoned.

### Priority: Medium

**4. Assessment Renaming**

| Current | Proposed |
|---------|----------|
| ADHD Screen | Focus & Organization Style |
| Cognitive Style | Pattern Recognition & Detail Sensitivity |
| Reasoning I/II/III | Logic Puzzles I/II/III |
| Shadow Self → Detachment | Shadow Self → Cold Focus |

**5. Assessment-to-Question Bridge**
Add narrative transition when users move from world questions to assessments:
> "The questions asked about the world. Now let's ask about YOU."

**6. Big Five Restructuring**
Break 50-item Big Five into 5 named mini-tests with individual results:
- Social Battery (Extraversion, 10 items)
- The Peacekeeper (Agreeableness, 10 items)
- Chaos Coordinator (Conscientiousness, 10 items)
- The Worry Meter (Neuroticism, 10 items)
- The Explorer (Openness, 10 items)

### Priority: Future

**7. Netflix-Style Assessment Unlocking** — show 3-5 at a time, not all 17

**8. Micro-Rewards** — "You're trending toward ___" after 5-8 answers

**9. Viral Loop UX** — "Share your answer" button, "Compare with a friend," "X% of people agree with you"

**10. "Spicy Takes" as Visible Category** — branded section for the cultural debates
