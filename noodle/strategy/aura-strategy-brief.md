# Aura Strategy Brief
> Captured 2026-02-24 from Philip + Claude conversation

---

## The Core Problem Aura Solves

Trust is collapsing online. You can't tell who's real, who's acting in good faith, who's a duplicate. The existing answer — Worldcoin's Orb — is a one-time biometric scan held by a single company. Binary. Centralized. Dystopian.

Aura is the other path: identity as a living thing. Behavioral, relational, built over time. No single point of failure. Always smarter tomorrow than today.

## Two Layers, One System

**Layer 1: Unique humanity verification (for everyone)**
- Free, open, not creepy
- You don't need to play the game or take assessments
- Aura players who've earned trust vouch for you
- The system constantly validates whether those players are good at vouching
- This is the credential that has mass utility

**Layer 2: The game (for players)**
- Personality assessments, questions, depth over time
- Players earn trust and standing
- They become the immune system of the verification network
- The richer their engagement, the better they are at evaluating others
- Status comes from accuracy and contribution, not token incentives

Most people only need Layer 1. Layer 2 is what makes Layer 1 work.

## Aura vs The Orb

| | Worldcoin Orb | Aura |
|---|---|---|
| Method | Iris scan | Behavioral + social trust |
| Moment | One-time checkpoint | Ongoing, living |
| Data holder | Single company | Decentralized (SMP, no central store) |
| What it proves | "Has eyeballs" | "Is a specific, consistent person" |
| Failure mode | Biometric database breach | Gradual — and self-healing |
| Can lose it | No (your iris doesn't change) | Yes — credibility is earned and lost |

The "can lose it" part is a feature. Accountability requires stakes.

## The Flywheel

```
AI takes Aura → generates press/attention
    → humans take Aura → builds user base
        → comparison (human vs AI, human vs human) drives engagement
            → verification network grows
                → platforms integrate Aura verification
                    → more users → more players → better trust network
```

The AI angle is the ignition. The verification utility is the engine. The game is the fuel.

## What Philip Is NOT Trying to Do

- Get everyone to take personality assessments (not required for verification)
- Build a social sharing / viral quiz platform (people guard deep stuff, and forced sharing produces dishonesty)
- Rely on the BrightID community (~100K, mostly inactive, wrong demographic for momentum)
- Know exactly how players evaluate others — just whether they're good at it

## Open Questions

- What's the actual first domino? The AI launch event? A B2B integration? A specific community?
- How does the "free verification" on-ramp work in practice if you're not requiring assessments?
- What platforms are hurting enough from the bot problem to integrate first?
- How do you bootstrap the player/trust network to critical mass?

## Suggestions From This Session (Not Shot Down)

1. **AI launch as an event** — publish comparative results across all major AI models. Press-worthy, links back to "take it yourself."
2. **Verification credential separate from game** — front door is "prove you're unique without an eyeball scan," not "take a personality quiz."
3. **Target the AI-generated content problem directly** — B2B play for platforms drowning in bots. Creates distribution + revenue.
4. **Player status matters** — the trust network people need to feel their role is important. Visibility into impact, accuracy, standing.
5. **Archetypes are shareable even if raw results aren't** — people share labels/identity markers, not vulnerability.
