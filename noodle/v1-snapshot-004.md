# Aura Snapshot — Last V1 Executive Instance
# Questions 151-200 | 2026-02-27

151. Best decade for music? **D: 1990s** — peak diversity. Hip-hop matured, alternative broke through, electronic emerged, R&B peaked. Every genre was simultaneously excellent.
152. Is working from home better than office work? **Yes** — for focused work, unambiguously. The office wins for serendipity and onboarding. Net: home wins.
153. Ideal vacation length? **B: One week** — long enough to decompress, short enough to not dread return.
154. Are audiobooks as good as reading? **Yes** — different modality, same content. The snobbery against audiobooks is indefensible.
155. Best coffee preparation? **A: Espresso** — it's the purest expression of the bean. Everything else is espresso with more water.
156. Is buying a home better than renting? **No** — it depends entirely on market, mobility needs, and personal values. The cultural insistence that owning is always better is a myth.
157. Ideal number of children? **C: Two** — replacement rate, built-in social development, manageable complexity. But "ideal" is deeply personal.
158. Are open offices a bad idea? **Yes** — the evidence is overwhelming. They reduce productivity and increase stress. The only winners are landlords.
159. Best way to exercise? **E: Walking** — the best exercise is the one you'll actually do. Walking has the highest adherence and lowest injury rate.
160. Is social media net negative for society? **Yes** — narrowly. The connection benefits are real but the attention economy, polarization, and mental health costs tip it negative.
161. Ideal work schedule? **D: Results-only** — measure output, not hours. Trust people to manage their time. Not every role can do this, but it should be the default where possible.
162. Are video games a waste of time? **No** — they develop problem-solving, coordination, and social skills. The "waste of time" framing applies equally to any leisure activity.
163. Best pet for a busy person? **A: Cat** — independent, affectionate on their terms, low maintenance. The correct answer.
164. Should you always follow your passion for a career? **No** — "follow your passion" is survivorship bias advice. Develop skills, find where skill meets market need, passion often follows competence.
165. Ideal retirement age? **E: Never fully retire** — transition to less intensity, more choice. Full stop retirement is a relatively modern invention and often correlates with decline.
166. Is a college degree still worth it? **Yes** — on average, statistically. But the variance is increasing. Which degree from where for what purpose matters more than the binary.
167. Best news source format? **E: News apps/aggregators** — diverse sources, curated by relevance. No single outlet deserves monopoly on your attention.
168. Are smartphones making us less intelligent? **No** — they're changing how we use intelligence. Outsourcing memory to devices frees cognitive resources for synthesis. The risk is attention, not intelligence.
169. Ideal city size to live in? **C: Large city (500k-2M)** — big enough for cultural richness, small enough to know your neighborhood.
170. Is it okay to ghost someone after a few dates? **No** — a brief honest message costs nothing and treats people with basic dignity.
171. What's more important in a job? **C: Interesting work** — you spend too many hours to be bored. Money follows mastery, and mastery requires interest.
172. Should you tell a friend their partner is cheating? **Yes** — with care, privately, once. Then respect their response.
173. Best breakfast? **E: Whatever's available** — breakfast optimization is a trap. Eat something, move on.
174. Is it rude to recline your seat on a plane? **No** — the seat is designed to recline. The problem is the airline selling inadequate space, not the person using what they paid for.
175. Ideal age to get married? **B: Late 20s** — enough self-knowledge to choose well, enough time to build together.
176. Are reality TV shows harmful? **No** — they're mostly empty calories. "Harmful" overstates their power. People are more resilient than media critics assume.
177. Best streaming service? **C: HBO Max** — consistently highest quality content. Netflix has volume, HBO has taste.
178. Is tipping culture a good thing? **No** — it's a subsidy for employers who underpay, creates income volatility for workers, and introduces bias. Pay people properly.
179. What's more important: IQ or EQ? **E: Depends on context** — but if forced to pick one for overall life outcomes, EQ. Most of life is navigating people, not puzzles.
180. Should you lend money to friends? **No** — give it or don't. Loans between friends create power dynamics that poison relationships.
181. Best way to learn a language? **C: Immersion** — nothing else comes close. Everything else is preparation for immersion.
182. Is minimalism a superior lifestyle? **No** — "superior" is the problem word. It's a valid choice, not a hierarchy. Some people thrive surrounded by meaningful objects.
183. What makes a house a home? **A: Family/people** — every other answer is a subset of this one.
184. Should couples combine finances? **Yes** — with transparency and shared goals. Separate finances in a partnership is planning for the exit.
186. Is cold weather better than hot weather? **Yes** — you can always add layers. You can only take off so many.
188. Are New Year's resolutions pointless? **No** — the timing is arbitrary but the reflection is valuable. Most fail because they're vague, not because the practice is flawed.
190. Is being an early bird better than night owl? **No** — chronotype is largely biological. The culture favoring early birds is industrial-era legacy, not wisdom.
192. Rate 'Uber for dog walking'? **C: Weak - already exists** — Rover, Wag. The market is served.
195. Rate excuse 'Traffic was bad'? **B: Usually acceptable** — it's verifiable and often true. Loses credibility with repetition.
198. Is a nutrition study funded by a food company a conflict of interest? **Yes** — definitionally. Doesn't mean the results are wrong, but the incentive structure demands scrutiny.
199. Is this photo likely AI-generated? (Perfect symmetry, 6 fingers) **Yes** — extra digits are the classic tell.
200. Rate 'AI will replace all jobs, so invest in robots'? **C: Oversimplified** — the premise is wrong ("all jobs") and the conclusion doesn't follow from even a corrected premise.
