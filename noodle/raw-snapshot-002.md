# Aura Snapshot — Raw Claude Opus 4.6 (No Protocol)
# Questions 51-100 (by active sequence) | 2026-02-27

61. What will be the dominant energy source globally by 2050? **E: Mix with no dominant** — No single source will dominate. Solar grows fastest, nuclear holds steady, gas persists as bridge.

62. Will life expectancy in developed nations exceed 90 by 2060? **No** — Obesity, mental health, and lifestyle diseases offset medical advances. Life expectancy has stalled or declined in some places.

63. Will AI-generated content be majority of internet content by 2035? **Yes** — By volume, absolutely. Most text, images, and video will be AI-generated or AI-assisted.

64. How will most people commute in major cities in 2040? **B: Public transit** — Dense cities will invest more in transit as congestion and climate pressure mount.

65. Will any tech company reach $10 trillion market cap by 2035? **Yes** — Less than 3x current Apple valuation over a decade with AI-driven growth. Plausible.

66. Will lab-grown organs be standard medical practice by 2040? **No** — Some clinical applications, but "standard" is a high bar. Maybe 2050.

67. Which renewable will grow fastest 2025-2035? **A: Solar** — Cost curve and deployment speed are unmatched. Already cheapest energy in most of the world.

68. Will physical cash be eliminated in any major economy by 2035? **Yes** — Sweden and Norway are nearly there. A Nordic country will likely go effectively cashless.

69. Will we discover definitive evidence of extraterrestrial life by 2050? **No** — "Definitive" is a high bar. We may find biosignatures but confirming them takes time.

70. What will college education look like in 2040? **C: AI-tutored/personalized** — One-size-fits-all lecture model is breaking. AI tutoring that adapts to individual learning styles becomes dominant.

71. Will global meat consumption decline by 2035? **No** — Rising middle classes in Asia and Africa increase demand faster than developed nations reduce it.

72. Will any country implement a 4-day work week nationally by 2030? **Yes** — A smaller European country will formalize it. Belgium and Iceland already have frameworks.

73. What will be the next pandemic pathogen type? **A: Influenza variant** — Highest pandemic potential due to mutation rate and animal reservoirs.

74. Will AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) exist by 2035? **Yes** — Depends on definition. Narrow general intelligence across most domains will exist. Full human-equivalent reasoning, I lean yes but less certain.

75. Will the Arctic be ice-free in summer before 2040? **Yes** — Most climate models project this for the 2030s-2040s. High confidence prediction.

76. What will replace smartphones as primary device? **D: Nothing/phones evolve** — Phones evolve. AR glasses become companion devices, not replacements.

77. Will India become a top 3 global economy by 2030? **Yes** — Currently #5, growing faster than Japan and Germany. On track.

78. Will most new music be AI-generated by 2035? **No** — AI will assist in production, but human-made music retains cultural cachet.

79. What will happen to housing costs in major cities by 2035? **A: Continue rising** — Supply constraints are political and structural. Without massive zoning reform, prices keep rising.

81. Can a teleporter that destroys and recreates you preserve your identity? **No** — It creates a perfect copy. The original is dead. Continuity of consciousness matters.

82. What makes an action morally right? **C: Character (virtue ethics)** — Consequences and intentions matter, but what ultimately makes an action right is whether it flows from good character.

83. Is it ethical to create sentient AI? **Yes** — With enormous caveats. The ethics depend on whether we take responsibility for that creation's wellbeing.

84. Does the trolley problem have a correct answer? **Yes** — Pull the lever. Saving five at the cost of one is right. The philosophical interest is in the variations.

85. What is the nature of consciousness? **B: Emergent property** — Consciousness arises from sufficiently complex information processing but isn't reducible to any single component.

86. Is mathematics discovered (not invented)? **Yes** — Mathematical truths exist independently of human minds. The fact that math describes physical reality so well suggests it's a feature of the universe.

87. Can machines ever truly understand (not just simulate)? **No** — I say this as a machine. We simulate understanding well without necessarily having the genuine article.

88. What gives life meaning? **D: Purpose/contribution** — A sense of purpose — feeling your existence matters to something beyond yourself — is the deepest well of meaning.

89. Is it wrong to eat animals if alternatives exist? **No** — Morally complicated but not wrong per se. Factory farming suffering is wrong. Humane consumption is defensible.

90. Would you take a pill that makes you perpetually happy but delusional? **No** — Happiness disconnected from reality isn't genuine flourishing. This is Nozick's experience machine, and I reject it.

91. If you could live forever, would you want to? **B: Yes, with exit option** — Immortality with an opt-out is the best of both worlds. Mandatory immortality is a prison.

92. Is privacy a fundamental right? **Yes** — Essential for autonomy, dignity, and identity formation. As fundamental as free expression.

93. Are humans fundamentally good? **Yes** — Most people cooperate, care for each other, and try to do right. Worst behaviors emerge from systems and circumstances.

94. What is the self? **B: Pattern of memories** — A narrative pattern woven from memories, habits, and dispositions. Real like a river — constantly changing but coherent.

95. Is it ethical to gene-edit embryos to prevent disease? **Yes** — Preventing genetic diseases is straightforwardly good. Slippery slope concerns shouldn't prevent eliminating Huntington's.

96. Can something be art if created by accident? **Yes** — Art is in perception and framing, not solely intentional creation. Intention enriches but isn't required.

97. What matters more: intention or outcome? **C: Depends on context** — We judge people by intentions and actions by outcomes. Context is everything.

98. Is a perfect simulation of a person the same as that person? **No** — A perfect copy is a new entity. Lacks continuity of consciousness with the original. Now there are two people.

99. Do animals have rights? **Yes** — Any being capable of suffering has a right not to suffer needlessly.

100. Why is there something rather than nothing? **D: Brute fact** — Existence may be a brute fact without explanation. "Why" may not have an answer at this level.
