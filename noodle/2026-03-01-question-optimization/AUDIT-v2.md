# Aura Questions Database Audit v2 (2026-03-01)

## Executive Summary

The Aura question database contains **411 active questions** across 3 categories:
- **Predictions (84 q)**: Future-focused bets with resolution timelines
- **Reasoning (102 q)**: Philosophy & abstract thinking ("Looking out at the world")
- **Judgment (225 q)**: Opinion calls, ethics, lifestyle decisions

**Key Finding**: The database is MISALIGNED with Aura's actual purpose. Too many questions treat personality signal as the primary job, when the real 4 purposes are:
1. Entertainment/engagement (fun, shareable, debate-worthy)
2. Signal back to users (calibrated thinking, not personality traits)
3. Teach confidence mechanics (binary vs multi, Hunch→Certain spectrum)
4. Build track records (especially predictions that resolve, proving calibration)

**Result**: 30+ archive candidates. 20+ new questions needed. Significant restructuring recommended.

---

## 1. CATEGORY HEALTH CHECK

### Predictions (84 active) — **GOOD but uneven**
- **Serving purpose?** Mostly yes. But timeline distribution is skewed.
- **Right size?** 84 is good. But composition needs work.
- **What's working**: Near-term bets (Bitcoin $150k by July 2026, GPT-5 by Sept 2026) are excellent for fast resolution and track record building. Medium-term AI questions (AGI by 2035, GPT-5 adoption) tap real uncertainty.
- **What's not**: Too many vague/unresolvable questions (e.g., "Which company will dominate AI by 2030?" — what's domination?). Too many long-tail 2050 questions that won't resolve in user's lifetime. Missing sports, politics, culture predictions entirely.

**Recommended size:** 80-90 (trim vague, add near-term)

### Reasoning (102 active) — **OVERSIZED, identity-confused**
- **Serving purpose?** Partially. These feel like personality probes, not "looking out at the world."
- **Right size?** No. 102 is bloated. Many overlap.
- **What's working**: Engaging philosophical thought experiments (teleporter identity, trolley problem, Ship of Theseus) teach reasoning and are debate-worthy.
- **What's not working**:
  - 30+ straight ethics/morality questions (Is it ethical to X? Is it wrong to Y?) — monotonous, not shareable, don't build calibration
  - Pure "what is X" metaphysical questions (What is consciousness? What is truth?) — interesting but no clear answer frame, low engagement
  - Reads like a personality test trying to mine values, not teaching opinion mechanics

**Recommended size:** 60-70 (halve the pure ethics questions, keep creative thought experiments)

### Judgment (225 active) — **MASSIVE, but wildly misfit**
- **Serving purpose?** Mixed. This is where the real problems live.
- **Right size?** No. 225 is excessive. And poorly stratified.
- **What's working**:
  - Calibration/critical thinking questions (Q191-215: "Is this headline clickbait?", "Is this claim verifiable?", "Is this source credible?") — EXCELLENT for future vision (evaluating real claims, reviews, pitches)
  - Divergent opinion/fun questions (Q441-485: toilet paper direction, GIF pronunciation, pineapple pizza) — highly engaging, shareable, teach confidence mechanics
  - Political/policy questions (Q116-358) — real opinions, debate-worthy

- **What's not working**:
  - 60+ micro-personal lifestyle questions (Q152-223, Q361-421): "What's the ideal vacation length?", "Is cold weather better?", "What's the best breakfast?" — These are NOT about looking out at the world; they're personality/preference mining. Low engagement, not shareable, don't teach calibration. They're filler.
  - Archived questions still in the database creating noise (10+ dead questions flagged x:1 still appearing in lists)
  - Redundant opinion clusters (3+ questions on work-from-home, 4+ on dating/marriage age, 5+ on education)

**Recommended size:** 150-170 (cut 60+ lifestyle filler, consolidate redundancy)
