# Personality & Psychological Assessment Landscape Catalog

A comprehensive mapping of validated and popular personality, psychological, and wellbeing assessments available worldwide. This catalog documents the full spectrum of assessment tools to identify opportunities, gaps, and insights for question-based personality assessment design.

**Last Updated:** March 1, 2026

---

## 1. MAJOR VALIDATED PERSONALITY FRAMEWORKS

### 1.1 Big Five / OCEAN (Five-Factor Model)
- **What It Measures:** Five fundamental personality dimensions: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism
- **Validation Status:** Gold standard in personality psychology; most researched personality construct worldwide
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes, extensively validated across 30+ diverse cultures
- **Typical Item Count:** 10-300 items depending on version (NEO-PI-R has multiple formats)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Mix - many public domain versions available (IPIP NEO), proprietary versions (Costa & McCrae NEO-PI-R)
- **Popularity:** Universal in academic psychology, organizational psychology, and HR
- **Sources:**
  - [Big Five personality traits - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Five_personality_traits)
  - [Using the Big Five Personality Traits (OCEAN) in Practice](https://positivepsychology.com/big-five-personality-theory/)

### 1.2 HEXACO Model
- **What It Measures:** Six personality dimensions - Humility-Honesty (H), Emotionality (E), eXtraversion (X), Agreeableness (A), Conscientiousness (C), Openness to Experience (O)
- **Validation Status:** Scientifically validated; growing support in academic community
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes, with strong psychometric properties
- **Typical Item Count:** 200, 100, 60-item versions available; HEXACO Adjective Scales (HAS) has 60 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Mix - some proprietary versions available at hexaco.org
- **Popularity:** Less universal than Big Five but gaining traction; particularly valued for measuring dark personality traits via the Honesty-Humility dimension
- **Note:** The sixth trait (Honesty-Humility) is unique and particularly valuable for detecting unethical tendencies
- **Sources:**
  - [HEXACO Personality Inventory - Revised](https://hexaco.org/scaledescriptions)
  - [HEXACO model of personality structure - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HEXACO_model_of_personality_structure)

### 1.3 Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)
- **What It Measures:** 16 personality types based on four dichotomies (Introversion/Extraversion, Sensing/Intuition, Thinking/Feeling, Judging/Perceiving)
- **Validation Status:** Controversial - extensively used but criticized by scientific community
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Claimed to meet scientific standards, but validity disputed by many psychologists
- **Typical Item Count:** Varies (official instrument is proprietary)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary - The Myers-Briggs Company controls official assessment
- **Popularity:** Extremely popular in business, coaching, and public culture; heavily used on social media (55% YoY increase in social discussion 2024)
- **Critical Note:** Widely regarded as pseudoscience by psychology academics despite commercial success
- **Sources:**
  - [MBTI Assessment - Myers-Briggs Type Indicator Tool](https://www.themyersbriggs.com/en-US/Explore-Solutions/MBTI)
  - [Myers–Briggs Type Indicator - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myers%E2%80%93Briggs_Type_Indicator)

### 1.4 Enneagram
- **What It Measures:** Nine personality types with core motivations, fears, and internal dynamics
- **Validation Status:** Pop psychology; not scientifically validated
- **Peer-Reviewed:** No substantial peer-reviewed validation
- **Typical Item Count:** Varies by platform; typically 40-100 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Mix of free and proprietary versions
- **Popularity:** Very popular in wellness, self-development, and spiritual communities; growing popularity especially among Gen Z
- **Sources:**
  - [Enneagram Type Descriptions - The Enneagram Institute](https://www.enneagraminstitute.com/type-descriptions/)

### 1.5 DISC Assessment
- **What It Measures:** Four behavioral styles - Dominance, Influence, Steadiness, Conscientiousness
- **Validation Status:** Disputed - providers claim scientific validation; psychologists question predictive validity
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Limited peer-reviewed evidence; many studies show low predictive validity for job performance
- **Typical Item Count:** Varies; typically 24-30+ items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Mostly proprietary (TTI SI, Inscape, others)
- **Popularity:** Popular in business and organizational development (50 years of use); limited academic support
- **Critical Note:** No demonstrated ability to predict job performance despite widespread commercial use
- **Sources:**
  - [What is the DiSC assessment? - DiSC Profile](https://www.discprofile.com/what-is-disc)
  - [DISC Assessment - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DISC_assessment)

## 2. CLINICAL SCREENING TOOLS

### 2.1 ADHD Screening

#### Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale (ASRS-5)
- **What It Measures:** DSM-5 ADHD symptoms in adults
- **Validation Status:** Validated; excellent diagnostic accuracy (AUC 0.904)
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 6-item screener (Part A); 18-item full version
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary (WHO/Harvard)
- **Popularity:** Standard screening tool in clinical and medical settings
- **Recent Update:** Scoring changed in 2024 from dichotomous to 5-point Likert scale for enhanced accuracy
- **Sources:**
  - [The ASRS-5 | Embrace Autism](https://embrace-autism.com/asrs-5/)
  - [Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale (ASRS)](https://novopsych.com/assessments/diagnosis/adult-adhd-self-report-scale-asrs/)

### 2.2 Depression & Anxiety Screening

#### Patient Health Questionnaire-9 (PHQ-9)
- **What It Measures:** Depression severity
- **Validation Status:** Formally validated; sensitivity 88%, specificity 88% at cutoff ≥10
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes, extensively
- **Typical Item Count:** 9 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain (Pfizer)
- **Popularity:** Standard in primary care, medical settings, and research
- **Scoring:** 0-27 scale; categories from mild to severe
- **Sources:**
  - [PHQ-9 and GAD-7 — Medi-Stats](https://www.medi-stats.com/gad-7-phq-9)

#### Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7 (GAD-7)
- **What It Measures:** Generalized anxiety disorder severity
- **Validation Status:** Formally validated; sensitivity 89%, specificity 82% at cutoff ≥10
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 7 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Standard in clinical and research settings
- **Note:** Also screens well for panic disorder, social anxiety, and PTSD
- **Sources:**
  - [Validation of PHQ-9 and GAD-7](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8794093/)

### 2.3 Autism Spectrum Screening

#### AQ-10 (Autism Spectrum Quotient)
- **What It Measures:** Autistic traits; screens for need for full autism assessment
- **Validation Status:** Validated; recommended by NICE guidelines
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 10 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Standard primary care screening tool in UK and internationally
- **Sources:**
  - [The AQ-10 | Embrace Autism](https://embrace-autism.com/aq-10/)

#### RAADS-R (Ritvo Autism Asperger Diagnostic Scale–Revised)
- **What It Measures:** Autistic traits in adults (especially those missed in childhood)
- **Validation Status:** Validated; 97% sensitivity
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 80 items with 4-point scale measuring traits "now" vs "when younger"
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary
- **Popularity:** Increasingly popular for adult autism identification
- **Advantage:** Provides developmental context unlike AQ-10
- **Sources:**
  - [RAADS–R | Embrace Autism](https://embrace-autism.com/raads-r/)

### 2.4 OCD Screening

#### Yale-Brown Obsessive-Compulsive Scale (Y-BOCS)
- **What It Measures:** OCD symptom severity and treatment response
- **Validation Status:** Gold standard; reliability and validity well-established since 1989
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes, extensively
- **Typical Item Count:** 10 items for severity scoring (clinician-rated); 16 items with symptom list
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Industry standard for OCD assessment and research
- **Note:** Score of 16+ suggests probable OCD diagnosis
- **Sources:**
  - [Yale–Brown Obsessive–Compulsive Scale - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yale%E2%80%93Brown_Obsessive%E2%80%93Compulsive_Scale)

### 2.5 PTSD Screening

#### PTSD Checklist for DSM-5 (PCL-5)
- **What It Measures:** DSM-5 PTSD symptoms (all 20 criteria)
- **Validation Status:** Well-validated; internal consistency .83-.97, test-retest .58-.91
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 20 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain (VA)
- **Popularity:** Standard trauma screening tool in medical and mental health settings
- **Scoring:** Total 0-80; cutoff 31-33 suggests probable PTSD
- **Sources:**
  - [PTSD Checklist for DSM-5 (PCL-5) - PTSD: National Center for PTSD](https://www.ptsd.va.gov/professional/assessment/adult-sr/ptsd-checklist.asp)

### 2.6 Bipolar Disorder Screening

#### Mood Disorder Questionnaire (MDQ)
- **What It Measures:** Lifetime manic/hypomanic symptoms
- **Validation Status:** Mixed validation results; sensitivity varies by population
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 15 items (13 yes/no + 2 about co-occurrence and impairment)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Standard bipolar screening tool in clinical settings
- **Note:** Better at differentiating bipolar from unipolar depression than identifying bipolar in general populations
- **Sources:**
  - [Mood Disorder Questionnaire (MDQ)](https://novopsych.com/assessments/diagnosis/mood-disorder-questionnaire-mdq/)

## 3. RELATIONSHIP & ATTACHMENT ASSESSMENTS

### 3.1 Attachment Styles

#### Attachment Style Questionnaire - Short Form (ASQ-SF)
- **What It Measures:** Adult attachment patterns (secure, anxious, avoidant, fearful-avoidant)
- **Validation Status:** Validated for clinical and non-clinical assessment
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 29 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Research use available
- **Popularity:** Standard in relationship psychology and therapy
- **Dimensions:** Avoidance and Anxiety scales
- **Note:** Recent 2024 research shows insecure attachment correlates with reduced marital satisfaction
- **Sources:**
  - [Attachment Style Questionnaire – Short Form (ASQ-SF)](https://novopsych.com/assessments/formulation/attachment-style-questionnaire-short-form-asq-sf/)

### 3.2 Love Languages

#### The 5 Love Languages Assessment (Chapman)
- **What It Measures:** Primary love language preferences - Words of Affirmation, Acts of Service, Receiving Gifts, Quality Time, Physical Touch
- **Validation Status:** Pop psychology; mixed research support
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Limited peer review; recent 2024 study found mixed results
- **Typical Item Count:** Usually 30 items (10 min - 15 min completion)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary (Chapman/Northfield Publishers)
- **Popularity:** Extremely popular in relationship counseling and self-help
- **Critical Finding:** 2024 research suggests Words of Affirmation and Quality Time better predict satisfaction than primary love language alignment
- **Sources:**
  - [Discover Your Love Language® - The 5 Love Languages](https://5lovelanguages.com/)
  - [Testing the predictions of Chapman's five love languages theory](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39420529/)

### 3.3 Conflict Resolution Styles

#### Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument (TKI)
- **What It Measures:** Five conflict-handling modes - Competing, Collaborating, Compromising, Avoiding, Accommodating
- **Validation Status:** Validated; 40+ years of research
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 30 items (15-minute completion)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary (The Myers-Briggs Company)
- **Popularity:** Industry standard in organizational development, mediation, and coaching
- **Dimensions:** Assertiveness and Cooperativeness
- **Sources:**
  - [Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas%E2%80%93Kilmann_Conflict_Mode_Instrument)

## 4. COGNITIVE & THINKING ASSESSMENTS

### 4.1 Cognitive Reflection

#### Cognitive Reflection Test (CRT)
- **What It Measures:** Ability to resist intuitive but incorrect responses; reflects thinking style
- **Validation Status:** Validated but with limitations; somewhat controversial in scope
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 3 items (original); extended versions available
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Widely used in decision-making and behavioral economics research
- **Note:** Confounds reflection ability with mathematical ability; limited psychometric properties
- **Sources:**
  - [Cognitive reflection test - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_reflection_test)
  - [Cognitive Reflection Test (CRT)](https://sjdm.org/dmidi/Cognitive_Reflection_Test.html)

### 4.2 Thinking Styles

#### Sternberg Thinking Styles (Theory of Mental Self-Government)
- **What It Measures:** Cognitive style dimensions based on government functions (legislative, executive, judicial) and forms (monarchic, hierarchic, oligarchic, anarchic)
- **Validation Status:** Theoretically validated
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** Varies by instrument
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Mix
- **Popularity:** Used in educational assessment and cognitive style research
- **Related:** Also includes Triarchic Theory of Intelligence (Analytical, Creative, Practical)
- **Sources:**
  - [Thinking Styles — Robert J. Sternberg](http://www.robertjsternberg.com/thinking-styles)

### 4.3 Learning Styles

#### VAK/VARK Learning Styles
- **What It Measures:** Preferred learning modalities - Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic (and Reading/Writing in VARK version)
- **Validation Status:** Popular but NOT scientifically validated
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Limited; many studies show no benefit to matching instruction to learning styles
- **Typical Item Count:** 30 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Mostly public domain
- **Popularity:** Extremely popular in education despite lack of evidence
- **Critical Note:** There is no consistent evidence that better student outcomes result from identifying individual learning styles
- **Sources:**
  - [VARK Questionnaire: How do you learn best?](https://vark-learn.com/the-vark-questionnaire/)
  - [Learning styles - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learning_styles)

## 5. VALUES & MORAL ASSESSMENTS

### 5.1 Personal Values

#### Schwartz Portrait Values Questionnaire (PVQ)
- **What It Measures:** Individual value priorities across 10 (or 19 in refined version) universal value types
- **Validation Status:** Cross-culturally validated in 49+ cultural groups
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes, extensively
- **Typical Item Count:** 40 items (PVQ-40); newer refined version (PVQ-RR) measures 19 values
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Research use available
- **Popularity:** Standard in cross-cultural values research and HR
- **Method:** Asks respondent to compare themselves with short portrait descriptions rather than rating abstract values
- **Sources:**
  - [About the Schwartz Portrait Values Questionnaire (PVQ) – Rosemounts Institute](https://rosemounts.org/about-the-schwartz-portrait-values-questionnaire-pvq/)

### 5.2 Moral Reasoning

#### Moral Foundations Questionnaire (MFQ)
- **What It Measures:** Five moral foundations (Care, Fairness, Loyalty, Authority, Purity)
- **Validation Status:** Original MFQ-1 had reliability concerns; revised MFQ-2 has improved validity
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 30 items (MFQ-30)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Standard in moral psychology research
- **Note:** MFQ-2 (2023) shows significant improvements over original version
- **Sources:**
  - [Moral Foundations Questionnaire and Moral Foundations Sacredness Scale](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10376904/)
  - [Questionnaires | Moral Foundations Theory](https://moralfoundations.org/questionnaires/)

## 6. EMOTIONAL & SOCIAL INTELLIGENCE

### 6.1 Emotional Intelligence

#### Mayer-Salovey-Caruso Emotional Intelligence Test (MSCEIT)
- **What It Measures:** Ability-based emotional intelligence across four branches: perceiving, using, understanding, regulating emotions
- **Validation Status:** Highly validated; most researched EI measure
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes (1,500+ citations)
- **Typical Item Count:** 141 items (8 tasks)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary
- **Popularity:** Industry standard for EI assessment in research and practice
- **Note:** Doesn't correlate highly (.18-.21) with self-report EI measures (e.g., Bar-On EQ-i), measuring different constructs
- **Sources:**
  - [Mayer-Salovey-Caruso Emotional Intelligence Test (MSCEIT)](https://www.eiconsortium.org/measures/msceit.html)

### 6.2 Social Style

#### SOCIAL STYLES Model (TRACOM)
- **What It Measures:** Four observable communication and interaction styles - Analytical, Driver, Expressive, Amiable
- **Validation Status:** Theoretically validated; 40+ years of research
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Limited peer review but widely accepted in practice
- **Typical Item Count:** Varies; typically 20-30 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary (TRACOM)
- **Popularity:** Standard in organizational and communication training
- **Dimensions:** Assertiveness (task vs. relationship) and Responsiveness (ask vs. tell)
- **Note:** Focuses on observable behaviors rather than personality per se
- **Sources:**
  - [TRACOM's SOCIAL STYLE Model™](https://tracom.com/social-style-training/model)

## 7. MOTIVATION & DRIVE ASSESSMENTS

### 7.1 Grit & Persistence

#### Grit Scale (Duckworth)
- **What It Measures:** Passion and perseverance toward long-term goals
- **Validation Status:** Validated with predictive power
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 8 items (Grit-S, short form); 12 items (original)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Popular in education, military, and organizational settings
- **Predictive Validity:** Predicts academic performance, military retention, teacher effectiveness
- **Note:** Originally designed for individual differences, uncertain validity for measuring pre-post changes
- **Sources:**
  - [Grit Scale - Angela Duckworth](https://angeladuckworth.com/grit-scale/)
  - [Development and validation of the short grit scale (grit-s) - PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19205937/)

### 7.2 Growth Mindset

#### Growth Mindset Scale (Dweck)
- **What It Measures:** Belief that intelligence and abilities can be developed through effort
- **Validation Status:** Validated with strong psychometric properties
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 3 items (primary scale); 1-item alternative for severe time constraints
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Ubiquitous in education; increasingly used in organizations
- **Validation:** High internal consistency (.94-.98) and test-retest stability (.80 at 2 weeks)
- **Independence:** Largely independent of intelligence and optimism
- **Sources:**
  - [Growth Mindset Scale | SPARQtools](https://sparqtools.org/mobility-measure/growth-mindset-scale/)
  - [Measuring Growth Mindset: Validation of a Three-Item and a Single-Item Scale](https://econtent.hogrefe.com/doi/10.1027/1015-5759/a000735)

### 7.3 Self-Determination Theory Assessment

#### Autonomy/Competence/Relatedness Measures
- **What It Measures:** Intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation; autonomous motivation; satisfaction of three basic psychological needs
- **Validation Status:** Theoretically validated; widely used in research
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** Varies by specific scale; typically 10-30 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Mix
- **Popularity:** Standard in motivation research, particularly in education and organizational psychology
- **Theory:** Distinguishes between intrinsic motivation and different forms of extrinsic motivation (external regulation, introjection, identification, integration)
- **Sources:**
  - [Intrinsic and extrinsic motivation from a self-determination theory perspective](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0361476X20300254)

### 7.4 Proactive Personality

#### Proactive Personality Scale (Bateman & Crant)
- **What It Measures:** Disposition toward taking action, scanning for opportunities, and persevering through obstacles
- **Validation Status:** Validated with psychometric evidence
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 17 items (full); 6-item (PPS-6); 4-item (short form)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Mix
- **Popularity:** Used in organizational psychology and entrepreneurship research
- **Scoring:** 7-point Likert scale
- **Sources:**
  - [The Proactive Personality Scale – EMERGE](https://emerge.ucsd.edu/r_ymqnqptjjnf1jqr/)

## 8. DARK PERSONALITY TRAITS

### 8.1 Dark Triad

#### Dark Triad Assessments
- **What It Measures:** Narcissism, Machiavellianism, Psychopathy (subclinical versions)
- **Validation Status:** Multiple validated instruments
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** Varies - 27 items (SD3), 12 items ("Dirty Dozen")
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Mix (some public domain, some proprietary)
- **Popularity:** Growing research interest; used in organizational and social psychology
- **Individual Instruments:**
  - Narcissistic Personality Inventory (NPI)
  - Machiavellianism-IV (MACH-IV)
  - Self-Report Psychopathy Scale (SRP-III)
- **Sources:**
  - [Dark triad - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dark_triad)
  - [Dark Triad | Psychology Today](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/basics/dark-triad)

### 8.2 Dark Tetrad (Dark Triad + Sadism)

#### Short Dark Tetrad (SD4)
- **What It Measures:** Narcissism, Machiavellianism, Psychopathy, Sadism (everyday cruelty)
- **Validation Status:** Validated as screening instrument
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 28 items (SD4)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Emerging assessment as research on sadism increases
- **Note:** Sadism is the tendency to derive pleasure from others' pain; distinct from psychopathy where harm is indifferent
- **Additional Sadism Scales:** SSIS, VAST, ASP, CAST
- **Sources:**
  - [The Dark Tetrad: analysis of profiles and relationship with the Big Five personality factors](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-024-55074-w)
  - [Dark Tetrad | Psychology Today](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/basics/dark-tetrad)

### 8.3 Low Honesty-Humility (HEXACO)

#### HEXACO Honesty-Humility Subscale
- **What It Measures:** Tendency toward sincerity, fairness, and modesty vs. manipulativeness, dishonesty, greed
- **Validation Status:** Validated as part of HEXACO model
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** As part of HEXACO-PI-R (4 subscales within H dimension)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** See HEXACO in Section 1.2
- **Popularity:** Particularly valuable for detecting dark personality traits
- **Key Finding:** Strongly negatively correlated with Dark Triad (especially Machiavellianism)
- **Sources:**
  - [The HEXACO Honesty-Humility, Agreeableness, and Emotionality Factors](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1088868314523838)

## 9. WELLBEING & LIFE SATISFACTION

### 9.1 Life Satisfaction

#### Satisfaction with Life Scale (SWLS)
- **What It Measures:** Global cognitive judgment of life satisfaction
- **Validation Status:** Extensively validated across cultures, languages, ages, and contexts
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 5 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Standard in wellbeing research and positive psychology
- **Psychometrics:** Excellent internal consistency (.87) and test-retest reliability (.82 at 2 months)
- **Scoring:** Total 5-35; norms typically 23-28 for non-clinical
- **Measurement Invariance:** Validated across gender, age, countries (27 papers, 66,000+ respondents)
- **Sources:**
  - [Satisfaction with Life Scale (SWLS)](https://novopsych.com/assessments/well-being/satisfaction-with-life-scale-swls/)
  - [The Satisfaction With Life Scale - PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16367493/)

### 9.2 Resilience

#### Connor-Davidson Resilience Scale (CD-RISC)
- **What It Measures:** Ability to thrive in face of adversity
- **Validation Status:** Well-validated across diverse populations
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 25 items (full); 10-item and 2-item versions available
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Public domain
- **Popularity:** Standard trauma and resilience assessment
- **Dimensions:** Five factors identified through factor analysis
- **International Use:** Translated into many languages; used in community, clinical, military, elder, and trauma populations
- **Neuroimaging:** Included in functional neuroimaging and genetic studies
- **Sources:**
  - [Connor–Davidson Resilience Scale - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Connor%E2%80%93Davidson_Resilience_Scale)
  - [Development of a new resilience scale: the Connor-Davidson Resilience Scale (CD-RISC) - PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12964174/)

## 10. CHARACTER STRENGTHS & TALENTS

### 10.1 VIA Character Strengths

#### Values in Action Inventory of Strengths (VIA-IS)
- **What It Measures:** 24 character strengths organized under 6 universal virtues
- **Validation Status:** Validated; based on positive psychology research
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** Full version varies; typically 240 items for comprehensive assessment
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary (VIA Institute)
- **Popularity:** Growing in organizational and coaching contexts; increasing educational use
- **Signature Strengths:** Identifies top 5 strengths; 3-7 are typically signature strengths
- **Intervention Evidence:** Week-long interventions focusing on signature strengths show beneficial impact on happiness and depression
- **The 6 Virtues:** Wisdom, Courage, Humanity, Justice, Temperance, Transcendence
- **Sources:**
  - [24 Character Strengths List | VIA Institute](https://www.viacharacter.org/character-strengths)
  - [Mapping strengths into virtues: the relation of the 24 VIA-strengths to six ubiquitous virtues](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4404979/)

### 10.2 CliftonStrengths (StrengthsFinder)

#### CliftonStrengths 34 (Gallup)
- **What It Measures:** 34 talent themes organized in 4 domains - Executing, Influencing, Relationship Building, Strategic Thinking
- **Validation Status:** Validated based on 50+ years of research
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Limited academic peer review; extensive commercial validation
- **Typical Item Count:** Proprietary assessment (varies by version)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary (Gallup)
- **Popularity:** Extremely popular in organizations (467 Fortune 500 companies in 2015); 26 million+ people assessed
- **Business Orientation:** More business-focused than VIA; focuses on talent development
- **Domains:** Executing, Influencing, Relationship Building, Strategic Thinking
- **Sources:**
  - [What Are the 34 CliftonStrengths Themes? | EN - Gallup](https://www.gallup.com/cliftonstrengths/en/253715/34-cliftonstrengths-themes.aspx)

## 11. CREATIVITY & COGNITIVE ABILITY

### 11.1 Creativity Assessment

#### Torrance Tests of Creative Thinking (TTCT)
- **What It Measures:** Creative potential across fluency, flexibility, originality, elaboration
- **Validation Status:** Validated; most widely used creativity test globally
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** Multiple tasks (varies by form)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary (STSTESTING)
- **Popularity:** Standard in educational gifted assessment worldwide; often used in multicultural settings
- **Forms:** Verbal and Figural forms; originally called Minnesota Tests of Creative Thinking
- **Scoring Dimensions:** Fluency, Flexibility, Originality, Elaboration, plus Checklists of Creativity Strengths
- **Applications:** Gifted identification, entrance exams, special populations
- **Sources:**
  - [Torrance Tests of Creative Thinking - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torrance_Tests_of_Creative_Thinking)

## 12. EXECUTIVE FUNCTION & SELF-REGULATION

### 12.1 Executive Function

#### Behavior Rating Inventory of Executive Function - Adult Version (BRIEF-A)
- **What It Measures:** Everyday executive function and self-regulation across 9 clinical scales
- **Validation Status:** Validated; uses both self and informant report
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 75 items
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary (PAR)
- **Popularity:** Standard in neuropsychological assessment and ADHD evaluation
- **9 Scales:** Inhibit, Self-Monitor, Plan/Organize, Shift, Initiate, Task Monitor, Emotional Control, Working Memory, Organization of Materials
- **Indices:** Behavioral Regulation, Metacognition, Global Executive Composite
- **Versions:** BRIEF-P (preschool 2-5), BRIEF-SR (adolescent self-report 11-18), BRIEF-A (adult)
- **Clinical Applications:** ADHD, learning disabilities, ASD, TBI, MS, depression, cognitive impairment, dementia, schizophrenia
- **Sources:**
  - [Behavior Rating Inventory of Executive Function ® Adult Version](https://www.parinc.com/products/BRIEF-A)

## 13. CONTROL & AGENCY

### 13.1 Locus of Control

#### Rotter's Internal-External Locus of Control Scale
- **What It Measures:** Belief about controllability of outcomes (internal vs. external attribution)
- **Validation Status:** Validated since 1966; foundational construct
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** 29 items (23 scored + 6 filler); also IE-4 ultra-short (4 items, <30 seconds)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Mix (original public domain, some modern versions proprietary)
- **Popularity:** Fundamental construct in personality psychology; widely used in research
- **Scoring:** Forced-choice format; high score = external LOC, low score = internal LOC
- **Related Construct:** IE-4 is particularly useful for survey contexts with time/space constraints
- **Sources:**
  - [The Internal–External Locus of Control Short Scale–4 (IE-4): A comprehensive validation](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9273068/)

## 14. AUTHENTICITY & IMPOSTOR PHENOMENA

### 14.1 Impostor Syndrome/Phenomenon

#### Multiple Validated Scales
- **What It Measures:** Feelings of fraudulence, fear of being exposed as incompetent
- **Validation Status:** Multiple validated instruments
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Popularity:** Increasingly popular in academia and high-achievement populations
- **Major Instruments:**

**Clance Impostor Phenomenon Scale (CIPS)**
- Item Count: 20 items
- Scoring: Range 40-250; categories from few to intense IP experiences
- Status: Most widely used traditional measure

**Leary Impostorism Scale (LIS)**
- Item Count: Varies (typically 7 items in newer versions)
- Status: First unidimensional measure; allows overall score

**Brief Impostor Phenomenon Scale (BIPS)**
- Item Count: 12 items
- Subscales: Self-deprecation, External Ability Attribution, Inauthenticity
- Status: Shorter alternative to full scales

**Impostor Phenomenon Assessment (IPA)**
- Item Count: 72 items
- Factors: External Attribution, Negative Beliefs About Self, Self-Handicapping
- Status: Most comprehensive assessment

**Impostor Phenomenon Short Scale (IPSS-3)**
- Item Count: 3 items
- Status: Novel time-efficient universal measure for surveys and longitudinal studies

- **Sources:**
  - [Development, Factor Structure, and Psychometric Validation of the Impostor Phenomenon Assessment](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10478341/)
  - [Impostor Phenomenon Measurement Scales: A Systematic Review](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2019.00671/full)

## 15. ORGANIZATIONAL & CULTURAL ASSESSMENTS

### 15.1 Organizational Culture

#### Organizational Culture Assessment Instrument (OCAI)
- **What It Measures:** Organizational culture across 4 types (Clan, Adhocracy, Market, Hierarchy)
- **Validation Status:** Validated
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Yes
- **Typical Item Count:** Distributes 100 points across 4 statements (varies by version; typically 6 dimensions × 4 choices)
- **Public Domain/Proprietary:** Proprietary (Cameron & Quinn)
- **Popularity:** Widely used in organizational development and change management
- **Framework:** Based on Competing Values Framework (CVF) with 2 dimensions: Stability-Flexibility and Internal-External
- **Output:** Current culture profile vs. desired culture profile for gap analysis
- **Sources:**
  - [About the Organizational Culture Assessment Instrument (OCAI)](https://www.ocai-online.com/about-the-Organizational-Culture-Assessment-Instrument-OCAI)

## LANDSCAPE SUMMARY & INSIGHTS

### Assessment Categories Overview
1. **General Personality:** Big Five (gold standard), HEXACO, MBTI (popular but disputed), Enneagram, DISC
2. **Clinical Screening:** ASRS-5 (ADHD), PHQ-9/GAD-7 (mood), AQ-10/RAADS-R (autism), Y-BOCS (OCD), PCL-5 (PTSD), MDQ (bipolar)
3. **Relationship/Social:** Attachment (ASQ-SF), Love Languages, Conflict Styles (TKI), Social Styles (SOCIAL STYLES)
4. **Thinking/Cognition:** CRT (reflection), Sternberg Styles, VAK/VARK learning (popular but unvalidated)
5. **Values/Moral:** Schwartz PVQ (values), Moral Foundations Questionnaire
6. **Emotional/Social:** MSCEIT (EI), SOCIAL STYLES
7. **Motivation/Drive:** Grit Scale, Growth Mindset, Proactive Personality, Self-Determination Theory
8. **Dark Traits:** Dark Triad/Tetrad, HEXACO Honesty-Humility
9. **Wellbeing:** SWLS (life satisfaction), CD-RISC (resilience)
10. **Strengths:** VIA Character Strengths, CliftonStrengths
11. **Creativity:** TTCT (Torrance Tests)
12. **Executive Function:** BRIEF-A
13. **Control/Agency:** Locus of Control (Rotter, IE-4)
14. **Authenticity:** Impostor Syndrome scales (multiple)
15. **Organizational:** OCAI (culture assessment)
16. **Modern/Emerging:** AI-enhanced, digital alternatives, 16Personalities, OpenJung

### Validation Hierarchy
- **Gold Standard (Most Validated):** Big Five, HEXACO, SWLS, CD-RISC, MSCEIT, VIA, CliftonStrengths, TTCT, ASRS-5, PHQ-9, GAD-7, PCL-5, Y-BOCS, TKI
- **Well-Validated:** BRIEF-A, Grit Scale, Growth Mindset, Locus of Control, Moral Foundations (MFQ-2), CD-RISC, PVQ, Proactive Personality
- **Validated with Caveats:** DISC (business use popular but low job-performance predictivity), MBTI (commercially successful but scientifically disputed), Attachment (good theory, variable research), MDQ (population-dependent accuracy)
- **Pop Psychology (Limited Validation):** Enneagram, Love Languages (recent research mixed), VAK/VARK learning styles (popular but unvalidated)

### Item Count Range
- **Ultra-Short (1-4 items):** IE-4 LOC, IPSS-3 Impostor, Single-item Growth Mindset, CD-RISC-2
- **Short (5-10 items):** SWLS (5), GAD-7 (7), Grit-S (8), AQ-10 (10), CRT original (3-10)
- **Medium (15-30 items):** Big Five variants, HEXACO-60, ASRS (13-18), MDQ (15), MBTI variants, DISC variants, Enneagram (40-100)
- **Long (50+ items):** NEO-PI-R, HEXACO-200, BRIEF-A (75), IPA (72), MSCEIT (141), TTCT (multiple tasks)

### Public Domain vs. Proprietary
- **Fully Public Domain:** Big Five (many versions), HEXACO (some versions), GAD-7, PHQ-9, PCL-5, Y-BOCS, MDQ, CD-RISC, SWLS, Grit Scale, Growth Mindset, Proactive Personality, Locus of Control (Rotter), OCAI, IPIP, OEJTS
- **Proprietary:** MBTI, MSCEIT, CliftonStrengths, official VIA, Love Languages, DISC (various versions), SOCIAL STYLES, official Enneagram sources, TKI
- **Mixed:** HEXACO (some proprietary versions), Attachment instruments, Moral Foundations (original proprietary, MFQ-2 more open), TTCT

### Key Gaps & Opportunities
1. **Rapid Digital Assessment:** No dominant validated framework for ultra-short (3-5 item) personality assessment outside niche tools
2. **Integrated Assessment:** Limited tools combining personality, values, wellbeing, and motivation in single assessment
3. **Real-time Measurement:** Limited assessments designed for repeated micro-assessments or daily tracking
4. **Context-Specific:** Few assessments tailored to specific life domains (career, relationships, health, growth)
5. **Intersectionality:** Limited cultural adaptation beyond Big Five (HEXACO, PVQ are exceptions)
6. **Modern Life Factors:** Few assessments addressing digital-age personality traits (online behavior, social media engagement, remote work preferences, etc.)
7. **Longitudinal Design:** Most assessments not optimized for pre-post measurement of change
8. **Pop Psychology Validation:** Tools like Enneagram, MBTI remain popular despite scientific skepticism; gap between public interest and academic acceptance

### Trends (2024-2025)
- **Social Media Dominance:** MBTI discussions up 55% YoY; strong Gen Z engagement
- **AI Integration:** 70% of organizations predicted to use AI-based assessments by 2025
- **Short-Form Movement:** Increasing interest in ultra-short validated measures
- **Open Source Growth:** More open-source personality assessments appearing
- **Introverted Values:** Shift from exclusively celebrating extroversion toward recognizing introvert strengths
- **Holistic Wellbeing:** Increasing integration of traditional personality with wellbeing, resilience, and strengths measures
