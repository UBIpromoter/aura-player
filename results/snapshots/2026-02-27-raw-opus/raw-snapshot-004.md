# Questions 151-200 (by active sequence) | 2026-02-27

151. What's the best decade for music? **D: 1990s** — Peak diversity: grunge, hip-hop golden age, electronic music's rise, alternative rock. No other decade had as many thriving movements simultaneously.

152. Is working from home better than office work? **Yes** — For most knowledge workers, most of the time. Hybrid is the sweet spot.

153. What's the ideal vacation length? **C: Two weeks** — One week barely lets you decompress. Two weeks gives genuine rest and exploration.

154. Are audiobooks as good as reading? **Yes** — Different medium, comparable comprehension for narrative content. Neither is inherently superior.

155. What's the best coffee preparation? **A: Espresso** — Concentrated, versatile, and the purest expression of the bean's character.

156. Is buying a home better than renting? **No** — Depends entirely on location, market, duration, and opportunity cost. The "always buy" advice is cultural, not financial truth.

157. What's the ideal number of children? **C: Two** — Replacement rate, manageable, kids get a sibling. But any number including zero is valid.

158. Are open offices a bad idea? **Yes** — Evidence is clear: they reduce productivity, increase stress, and paradoxically reduce communication. They exist to save money on real estate.

159. What's the best way to exercise? **A: Gym/weights** — Resistance training has the broadest benefits: bone density, metabolism, injury prevention, mental health, longevity.

160. Is social media net negative for society? **Yes** — Benefits are real but harms (mental health, polarization, misinformation, attention destruction) are larger.

161. What's the ideal work schedule? **E: Varies by person** — The best schedule depends on the work, the person, and their life. Mandating one schedule for all is the real problem.

162. Are video games a waste of time? **No** — Games develop skills, provide social connection, and are a legitimate art form. Like any entertainment, only problematic when it crowds out everything else.

163. What's the best pet for a busy person? **A: Cat** — Independent, no walks needed, handle alone time well, still provide genuine companionship.

164. Should you always follow your passion for a career? **No** — Better: develop marketable skills, find meaningful work, let passion develop through mastery.

165. What's the ideal retirement age? **E: Never fully retire** — Gradually shift to part-time, volunteer, or passion projects. Total cessation correlates with cognitive decline.

166. Is a college degree still worth it? **Yes** — On average, the earnings premium is significant. But value depends heavily on field, school, and cost.

167. What's the best news source format? **E: News apps/aggregators** — Cross-reference multiple sources, get breaking news quickly, curate your diet. Print is best for deep analysis.

168. Are smartphones making us less intelligent? **No** — They're changing how we use intelligence. Real concern is attention fragmentation, not intelligence reduction.

169. What's the ideal city size to live in? **C: Large city (500k-2M)** — Big enough for culture and jobs, small enough to not be overwhelming. Austin, Portland, Copenhagen scale.

170. Is it okay to ghost someone after a few dates? **No** — A brief text is bare minimum courtesy. Ghosting is avoidance disguised as self-care. 30 seconds to be decent.

171. What's more important in a job? **C: Interesting work** — Beyond a money threshold, the work itself determines happiness. Interesting work creates flow and meaning.

172. Should you tell a friend their partner is cheating? **Yes** — Loyalty means telling hard truths. Silence is complicity.

173. What's the best breakfast? **E: Whatever's available** — The best breakfast is the one you actually eat. Fuel up and go.

174. Is it rude to recline your seat on a plane? **No** — The seat is designed to recline. Airlines sell you that feature. Blame the airline for cramming seats.

175. What's the ideal age to get married? **C: Early 30s** — Better self-knowledge, financial stability, lower divorce rates. Earlier can work but odds improve with experience.

176. Are reality TV shows harmful? **No** — Mostly harmless entertainment. Calling it harmful is paternalistic snobbery.

177. What's the best streaming service? **C: HBO Max** — Highest average quality of content. Netflix has volume; HBO has quality.

178. Is tipping culture a good thing? **No** — Shifts employer's wage responsibility onto customers. Creates income instability and bias. Pay people properly.

179. What's more important: IQ or EQ? **E: Depends on context** — IQ for technical problems; EQ for leadership and relationships. Most careers require both.

180. Should you lend money to friends? **No** — Give it as a gift or don't. Loans create power imbalances and resentment.

181. What's the best way to learn a language? **C: Immersion** — Nothing else comes close. Being forced to use a language all day accelerates learning faster than any alternative.

182. Is minimalism a superior lifestyle? **No** — One valid approach, not a moral high ground. What matters is intentionality, not the count.

183. What makes a house a home? **E: Feeling of safety** — Safety is the foundation. Everything else builds on that.

184. Should couples combine finances? **Yes** — At least partially. Joint for shared expenses, personal for discretionary spending. Partnership, not roommate dynamic.

186. Is cold weather better than hot weather? **Yes** — You can always add layers. Cold is more comfortable for activity, better for sleep, and no heat wave dangers.

188. Are New Year's resolutions pointless? **No** — The timing is arbitrary but periodic self-assessment is valuable. Set specific goals and they work.

190. Is being an early bird better than night owl? **No** — Chronotype is largely genetic. Neither is superior; society is structured for early birds, which is the real problem.
