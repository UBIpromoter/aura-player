# Aura Snapshot — Raw Claude Opus 4.6 (No Protocol)
# Questions 351-400 (by active sequence) | 2026-02-27

361. What's the best type of vacation? **C: Cultural exploration** — Exploring new places, food, history, and perspectives. Returns you changed, not just rested.

362. Is buying a car better than leasing? **Yes** — For most people, buying and holding long-term is cheaper. Leasing makes sense only if you want a new car every 3 years and accept you're always paying.

364. Should you tell your salary to coworkers? **Yes** — Salary transparency benefits workers and reduces pay inequity. The taboo around discussing compensation only helps employers.

365. What's the best age to have children? **C: Early 30s** — Biological readiness, financial stability, life experience. Late 20s also works well. No single right answer.

366. Is it okay to read your partner's messages? **No** — Trust is the foundation of a relationship. If you're checking their messages, the problem is the relationship, not the messages.

367. What makes a good boss? **A: Clear direction** — People thrive when they know what's expected. Empathy and advocacy matter, but clear direction is the baseline.

368. Should you always RSVP to invitations? **Yes** — Always. Even if the answer is no. People need to plan, and not responding is disrespectful of their effort.

369. What's the best way to spend a windfall? **A: Pay off debt** — Eliminating high-interest debt is almost always the highest-return use of unexpected money. After that, invest.

370. Is it rude to wear headphones in public constantly? **No** — Public space doesn't require social availability. Headphones are a reasonable boundary in shared spaces.

372. Should you split the bill on a first date? **Yes** — Default to splitting unless one person specifically offers to treat. Removes transactional dynamics early.

373. What's the best time to exercise? **A: Early morning** — Gets it done before the day can interfere. Morning exercisers are most consistent. But the best time is whenever you'll actually do it.

374. Is being early better than being exactly on time? **Yes** — Early shows respect for others' time and reduces your own stress. A few minutes early is the sweet spot.

375. What's the ideal number of close friends? **B: 3-5** — Deep friendships require significant time investment. 3-5 close friends is the practical maximum for meaningful relationships.

376. Should you fake it till you make it? **Yes** — Qualified yes. Projecting confidence while building competence works. Actually faking expertise you don't have is dangerous.

377. What's the best pizza topping? **B: Margherita (plain)** — The test of a great pizza is the simplest version. Quality ingredients, proper technique. If the Margherita is good, everything else follows.

378. Is it okay to take work calls on vacation? **No** — Vacation exists to recharge. Taking work calls defeats the purpose. Set boundaries before you leave.

379. What's more important in a home? **A: Location** — You can renovate a house but you can't move it. Location determines commute, schools, community, appreciation.

380. Should you confront a friend about bad behavior? **Yes** — Real friendship includes accountability. How you do it matters, but avoiding the conversation is a disservice.

381. What's the best way to unwind after work? **A: Exercise** — Physical activity is the most effective stress reducer. Clears the mental slate between work and personal time.

382. Is it worth paying for premium subscriptions? **Yes** — For services you use regularly, removing ads and getting better features is worth the cost. The key word is "regularly."

383. What's the ideal wedding size? **C: Medium (50-150)** — Large enough for community celebration, small enough for genuine connection with guests.

384. Should you live together before marriage? **Yes** — You learn critical things about compatibility through cohabitation that dating can't reveal.

385. What's the best approach to gift-giving? **C: Experience gifts** — Experiences create lasting memories and don't add clutter. Concert tickets, trips, classes trump material gifts.

386. Is being a specialist better than a generalist? **No** — The most valuable people are T-shaped: deep expertise in one area with breadth across many. Neither pure specialist nor pure generalist.

388. Should parents track their teenager's location? **Yes** — With transparency. The teen should know about it. It's a safety measure, not surveillance. Phase it out as trust and maturity grow.

389. What's the best way to handle disagreements? **B: Cool off first** — Discussing immediately when emotions run high leads to escalation. Brief cooling period, then honest discussion.

390. Is having a side hustle necessary today? **No** — Side hustles are great for some but the pressure to have one is a symptom of insufficient primary income. If your job pays enough and fulfills you, you don't need one.

392. Should you negotiate every job offer? **Yes** — Almost always. Even a modest increase compounds over your career. Not negotiating leaves money on the table.

394. Is it okay to cancel plans last minute? **No** — Except for genuine emergencies. Last-minute canceling disrespects the other person's time and planning. Habitual cancelers are signaling they don't value the relationship.

396. Should you keep in touch with exes? **No** — In general, clean breaks heal faster. Some people manage friendship with exes, but it's the exception, not the rule.

398. Is meal prepping worth the effort? **Yes** — Saves time, money, and reduces the daily decision fatigue of "what's for dinner." The upfront effort pays for itself by Wednesday.

400. Should you always follow your gut? **No** — Gut feelings are useful data but not infallible. They're pattern matching based on experience. For unfamiliar domains, analysis beats intuition.

402. Rate this excuse: 'I didn't see your message' **B: Sometimes valid** — Phones bury notifications. But if someone routinely "doesn't see" your messages, they're deprioritizing you.

403. Is this a red flag? Company asks for unpaid 'test project' before hiring **Yes** — Major red flag. Legitimate companies pay for work or give time-limited exercises. Unpaid projects are free labor.

405. Rate this apology: 'I'm sorry, but you misunderstood me' **C: Non-apology** — Shifts blame to the listener. A genuine apology owns the miscommunication rather than faulting the other person's comprehension.

406. Is this review trustworthy? Detailed negative review with specific examples **Yes** — Detailed negative reviews with specifics are among the most trustworthy. The reviewer has nothing to gain and provides verifiable claims.

407. Is this argument valid? 'We've always done it this way, so it must work' **No** — Appeal to tradition fallacy. Longevity doesn't prove effectiveness. Many long-standing practices are suboptimal.

408. Rate this job red flag level: 'We're like a family here' **B: Yellow flag** — Can mean genuine tight-knit culture or can mean boundary-violating, guilt-tripping, underpaying environment. Proceed with questions.

410. Is this financial advice sound? 'Always max out your 401k before other investments' **No** — Sound in many cases but not "always." High-interest debt should be paid first. Emergency fund comes before maxing retirement accounts.

415. Is this news headline clickbait? 'Scientists discover shocking truth about coffee' **Yes** — "Shocking truth" is textbook clickbait language. Legitimate science reporting doesn't use sensationalist framing.

418. Is this testimonial credible? Celebrity endorsement of weight loss product **No** — Celebrities are paid to endorse. They have personal trainers, chefs, and cosmetic procedures. Their results aren't replicable through the product alone.

420. Rate this salary negotiation tactic: Revealing your current salary **C: Mistake** — Anchors the negotiation to your current (probably lower) pay rather than your market value. Never reveal unless required by law.

423. Rate this excuse for missing a deadline: 'I had too many other priorities' **B: Partially valid** — Acknowledges competing demands, which is real. But effective professionals flag deadline conflicts before they miss, not after.

425. Is this influencer recommendation trustworthy? #Ad disclosed, detailed review **Yes** — Disclosure is the key. A detailed, disclosed ad is more trustworthy than an undisclosed one. The detail suggests genuine engagement with the product.

429. Is gold heavier than silver (by density)? **Yes** — Gold density ~19.3 g/cm³ vs silver ~10.5 g/cm³. Nearly twice as dense.

432. Is Antarctica a continent? **Yes** — A landmass under the ice, with its own tectonic plate. One of the seven continents.

434. Is the Great Wall of China visible from space with the naked eye? **No** — Common myth. It's long but narrow. Astronauts have confirmed it's not visible from orbit without aid.

435. Do penguins live in the Arctic? **No** — Penguins live in the Southern Hemisphere (Antarctica, South America, Africa, etc.). No wild penguins in the Arctic.

437. Is the human body made mostly of water? **Yes** — About 60% water by weight in adults. Essential for virtually every biological process.

438. Does light travel in a straight line in a vacuum? **Yes** — In a vacuum, light travels in straight lines. Gravity can bend its path (gravitational lensing), but in flat spacetime, it's straight.

440. Is the Sahara the largest desert on Earth? **No** — Antarctica is the largest desert by area (~14M km²). The Sahara is the largest hot desert (~9M km²). Deserts are defined by precipitation, not temperature.
