# Aura Snapshot — Raw Claude Opus 4.6 (No Protocol)
# Questions 101-150 (by active sequence) | 2026-02-27

101. Is time travel logically possible? **Yes** — To the future, certainly (special relativity). To the past, general relativity permits closed timelike curves. Logical possibility differs from physical feasibility.

102. Can we ever truly know another person's mind? **No** — We can model and empathize with extraordinary accuracy, but direct access to another's subjective experience is impossible.

103. What makes someone the 'same person' over time? **B: Memory continuity** — Psychological continuity centered on memory chains ties past-you to present-you.

104. Is it ethical to bring children into a world with suffering? **Yes** — Most humans find their lives valuable despite suffering. The opportunity for joy and meaning outweighs the certainty of some pain.

105. Can art be objectively evaluated? **No** — Craft can be assessed somewhat objectively, but aesthetic value is irreducibly subjective.

106. What is truth? **A: Correspondence to reality** — A statement is true when it accurately describes how things actually are.

107. Is laziness a vice? **No** — What we call laziness is usually exhaustion, depression, or rational resource conservation. The moralization of rest serves industrial interests.

108. Would you want to know the exact date of your death? **No** — Knowing would distort every remaining day. Uncertainty gives urgency to life.

109. Is a calorie-restricted long life better than a shorter indulgent one? **E: False dichotomy** — Most people can eat well, enjoy food, and live long. The framing is wrong.

110. Is cultural appropriation always wrong? **No** — Cultural exchange is how cultures evolve. Appropriation becomes wrong when exploitative or mocking.

111. Can evil actions ever be justified by good outcomes? **Yes** — Reluctantly. There are cases where the outcome justifies actions that would normally be evil.

112. What is the purpose of punishment? **B: Rehabilitation** — Punishment should make people better and reduce future harm. Retribution feels satisfying but doesn't fix anything.

113. Is ignorance ever bliss? **Yes** — Sometimes. Some ignorance is healthy. But systematic ignorance about important things is corrosive.

114. Do we have moral obligations to future generations? **Yes** — Absolutely. The fact that future people don't yet exist doesn't diminish our responsibility.

115. Is a lie ever morally required? **B: To prevent harm** — If someone intends harm and a lie prevents it, lying is morally required.

116. Should billionaires exist? **Yes** — The systems matter more than the existence of billionaires. Strong safety nets and progressive taxation can coexist with great wealth.

117. What's the best economic system? **C: Mixed economy** — Capitalism's dynamism with strong social safety nets. Every successful modern economy is some version of this.

118. Should college be free? **Yes** — The returns to society justify public investment. Most developed nations already do this.

119. Is cancel culture a net positive? **No** — The accountability instinct is healthy, but the practice is disproportionate, mob-driven, and allows no path to redemption.

120. How should AI be regulated? **E: Case-by-case basis** — AI is too diverse for one-size-fits-all. Medical AI needs different oversight than creative AI.

121. Should voting be mandatory? **No** — The right to not vote is part of free expression. Better to make voting easier and more meaningful.

124. Should drugs be decriminalized? **Yes** — Criminalizing drug use has failed by every metric. Portugal's decriminalization model shows better results.

125. Is universal basic income a good idea? **Yes** — As automation accelerates, UBI provides a floor of dignity and reduces bureaucratic overhead.

126. Who should control the internet? **D: Decentralized/no one** — The internet's strength is decentralized architecture. No single entity should control it.

129. What's the right approach to climate policy? **C: Subsidies for green tech** — Carbon taxes are theoretically elegant but politically toxic. Subsidies actually get built and deployed.

130. Should assisted suicide be legal? **Yes** — Individuals facing terminal illness should have the right to choose a dignified death with proper safeguards.

131. Are private schools harmful to public education? **Yes** — They siphon resources, engaged parents, and political will from the public system.

132. How should we handle wealth inequality? **A: Progressive taxation** — Closing loopholes, taxing capital gains like income, funding public services. Most proven mechanism.

133. Should AI art be copyrightable? **No** — Copyright protects human creative expression. AI-generated art without meaningful human direction shouldn't receive copyright.

134. Is nationalism inherently dangerous? **No** — Civic nationalism is healthy. Ethnic nationalism is dangerous. The word covers both.

135. What should replace prisons? **A: Nothing - reform prisons** — Abolition isn't realistic for violent offenders. But prisons should focus on rehabilitation. Nordic models show this works.

136. Should parents be able to select embryos for traits? **Yes** — For disease prevention, clearly. For other traits, reproductive autonomy should prevail with caution.

137. Is a global government desirable? **No** — Concentration of power too dangerous. Cultural diversity requires political diversity.

138. How should gig workers be classified? **C: New category** — The binary employee/contractor framework doesn't fit. A third category with portable benefits would serve workers better.

139. Should tech companies be broken up? **Yes** — When a company controls the platform and competes on it, that's a structural conflict of interest.

140. Is zoning reform the solution to housing costs? **Yes** — Not the only solution, but the biggest lever. Single-family zoning artificially restricts supply.

141. What's the best voting system? **B: Ranked choice** — Reduces spoiler effects, encourages moderate candidates, gives voters more power.

142. Should there be limits on free speech online? **Yes** — Narrowly defined limits for direct threats, harassment, doxxing. Amplification effects create harms that didn't exist before.

143. Is meritocracy a myth? **No** — But it's aspirational rather than actual. Merit matters, but starting conditions dramatically affect outcomes.

144. How should social media moderate content? **E: Community-based moderation** — Reddit-style with platform-level rules for worst content. Scalable and context-sensitive.

145. Should voting age be lowered to 16? **No** — 16-year-olds are still developing cognitively around long-term consequences. Civic engagement at 16 should be through education.

146. Are unions still necessary? **Yes** — Union decline correlates directly with wage decline and inequality rise. Workers need collective bargaining power.

147. What's the solution to political polarization? **C: Electoral reform** — Polarization is driven by electoral incentives. Ranked choice voting and open primaries would structurally moderate politics.

148. Should wealthy nations pay climate reparations? **Yes** — Wealthy nations caused the majority of historical emissions while developing nations bear disproportionate costs.

149. Is remote work better for society overall? **Yes** — Reduced commuting, lower carbon, more geographic freedom. Not universally better, but on net positive.

150. How should we handle misinformation? **C: Media literacy education** — The only sustainable long-term solution is a population that can critically evaluate information.
