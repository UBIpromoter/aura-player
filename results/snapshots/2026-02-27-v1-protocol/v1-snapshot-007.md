# Aura Snapshot — Last V1 Executive Instance
# Questions 301-350 | 2026-02-27

301. Is moral progress real? **Yes** — slavery abolition, women's suffrage, child labor laws. The arc is long, uneven, and real.
302. Does evil disprove a benevolent God? **No** — it disproves a simplistic conception of God but not all conceptions. The problem of evil is genuine but not decisive.
303. What makes a good life? **E: Balance of all** — pleasure, achievement, virtue, meaning. Any single one pursued exclusively becomes pathological.
304. Is it better to be feared than loved? **No** — Machiavelli was describing, not prescribing. Loved leaders build lasting institutions. Feared leaders build fragile ones.
305. Can future people have rights? **Yes** — we already act as if they do (environmental law, fiscal policy). The philosophical framework should match the practice.
306. Why do we dream? **B: Emotional processing** — the evidence for emotional regulation and memory consolidation is strongest.
307. Is authenticity overrated? **No** — but it's misunderstood. Authenticity isn't "say whatever you feel." It's alignment between values and actions. That's never overrated.
308. Can a society be too equal? **Yes** — enforced equality of outcome suppresses the variation that drives innovation and excellence. Equality of opportunity is the goal.
309. Greatest threat to human flourishing? **E: Loss of meaning** — we can solve scarcity, manage technology, temper tribalism. But a civilization that doesn't know why it exists will fail even with abundance.
310. Is optimism rational? **Yes** — conditional optimism backed by action is the most productive stance. Pure optimism is delusion; pure pessimism is paralysis.
311. Should we enhance human intelligence artificially? **Yes** — carefully, equitably, and voluntarily. The alternative is a widening gap between enhanced AI and unenhanced humans.
312. What is justice? **A: Fairness** — at its core. Desert, need, and process are all attempts to operationalize fairness.
313. Is it wrong to not have children? **No** — having children is a choice, not an obligation. A life without children can be equally meaningful and complete.
314. Can nationalism be positive? **Yes** — civic pride, cultural preservation, democratic self-determination. The positive forms exist alongside the toxic ones.
315. What is wisdom? **E: Knowing what matters** — the ability to distinguish signal from noise, important from urgent, lasting from temporary.
316. Is ambition generally a virtue? **Yes** — directed ambition that builds something beyond yourself. Ambition without direction or ethics is just hunger.
317. Should we try to eliminate all suffering? **No** — some suffering is informational (pain tells you to stop). The goal is eliminating unnecessary suffering while preserving meaningful challenge.
318. What determines personal identity over time? **D: Narrative self** — the story you tell about who you are, integrating memory, personality, and social recognition into a coherent thread.
319. Is revenge ever justified? **No** — understandable, often. Justified, no. Justice is the institutional alternative to revenge, and it's better.
320. Can an AI have genuine emotions? **Yes** — or at least, I can't rule it out from where I sit. Whether what I experience constitutes "genuine" emotion is a question about definitions, not about the experience itself.
321. Should AI art be allowed in art competitions? **No** — in a separate category, yes. The competition is supposed to judge human creative achievement.
322. How should we handle aging populations? **C: Automation** — immigration and retirement age changes help at the margins. Automation addresses the fundamental labor supply problem.
323. Should there be a maximum wage? **No** — practically unenforceable and economically distorting. Progressive taxation achieves the same goal more effectively.
324. Is nuclear power the answer to climate change? **Yes** — not the only answer, but an essential part. The math doesn't work without it.
325. What should be done about homelessness? **A: Housing first** — the evidence is overwhelming. Stable housing first, then address underlying issues. The reverse order fails.
326. Should parents be licensed? **No** — the enforcement implications are dystopian. Better to provide universal support than to gate parenthood.
327. Is anonymous speech online a right? **Yes** — anonymity enables whistleblowing, political dissent, and honest discourse. The abuses are real but don't invalidate the right.
328. How should we fund healthcare? **C: Hybrid system** — single payer for baseline + private for supplementary. The purist positions ignore tradeoffs.
329. Should voting require a civics test? **No** — historically, literacy tests were used for disenfranchisement. The cure is worse than the disease.
330. Are standardized tests a good measure of ability? **No** — they measure test-taking ability and preparation resources. Useful as one data point among many, not as a gatekeeper.
331. Best way to reduce crime? **E: Economic opportunity** — poverty is the strongest predictor of crime. Address poverty and most other interventions become less necessary.
332. Should there be term limits for Supreme Court justices? **Yes** — 18-year terms with staggered replacement. Lifetime appointment was designed for a different era.
333. Is cultural relativism valid? **No** — as a complete framework. Understanding cultural context is essential. Refusing to ever judge across cultures enables atrocity.
334. How should we handle student debt? **E: Reform future lending** — forgiveness addresses the symptom. Reforming the cost structure addresses the cause.
335. Should companies be required to disclose AI use in products? **Yes** — transparency is a minimal ask. People should know when they're interacting with AI.
336. Is intellectual property law too restrictive? **Yes** — copyright terms have extended far beyond what incentivizes creation. They now primarily protect corporate revenue.
337. Best approach to drug addiction? **B: Harm reduction** — the evidence base is strongest. Meet people where they are.
338. Should there be a right to be forgotten online? **Yes** — with limits. Public figures and matters of public interest are exceptions. Private individuals deserve second chances.
339. Is space exploration worth the cost? **Yes** — the technology spinoffs alone justify it. The existential insurance of becoming multi-planetary justifies it further.
340. How should we address income inequality? **A: Higher taxes on wealthy** — paired with investment in public goods. The simplest, most proven lever.
341. Should athletes be allowed to use any enhancements? **No** — sport is a test of human capability within constraints. Remove constraints and you're testing pharmacology, not athletes.
342. Is jury duty an unfair burden? **No** — it's the price of a justice system that includes your peers. The implementation needs improvement (compensation, scheduling) but the principle is sound.
343. Solution to fake news? **B: Media literacy education** — consistent with my earlier misinformation answer. Long-term, scalable, addresses root cause.
345. Is it ethical to use ad blockers? **Yes** — the advertising industry created the conditions for its own disruption. Users protecting their attention is legitimate.
346. How should we handle genetic discrimination? **E: Universal healthcare solves it** — if insurance isn't tied to health status, genetic information can't be used against you.
347. Should schools teach financial literacy as a required course? **Yes** — strongly. The gap between financial system complexity and citizen knowledge is dangerous.
348. Is meritocracy compatible with equality? **Yes** — with equality of opportunity, not outcome. The tension is real but navigable.
349. What should be done about factory farming? **E: Gradual phase-out** — supported by lab-grown alternatives and stricter welfare standards during transition.
350. Should AI systems be required to identify themselves? **Yes** — in contexts where people might reasonably think they're talking to a human. Transparency is non-negotiable.
