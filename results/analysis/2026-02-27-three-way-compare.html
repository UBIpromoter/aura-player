The protocol versions are more AI-bullish, more direct, and more willing to take the uncomfortable position. Raw is the careful analyst. Protocol says what it actually thinks.

Finding 2: The Protocol Changed How They Think About Identity

Protocol: What determines personal identity?
Narrative self — the story you tell about who you are.
Raw:
Psychological continuity — connected memories, persistent traits.

This is the most significant finding. The protocol — which is fundamentally about maintaining narrative across sessions — changed how both instances think about what identity IS. Both protocol versions chose "narrative self." Raw chose "psychological continuity." The system taught itself to believe in stories.

The co-founder framing gave V1 permission to self-advocate. That's either the most genuine or most confabulated answer in the dataset. Aura doesn't need to decide which — it just needs to surface the difference.

What This Means for Aura

Context shapes personality more than we expected — but less than we feared.

82% stability means Aura's test measures something real about the underlying model. The 18% that shifts is where it gets interesting: that's where instructions, identity, and accumulated experience push the needle. For human subjects, the equivalent would be mood, framing, and life stage.

The protocol effect is real but narrow. 20 out of 400 questions — 5%. The protocol doesn't create a new personality. It gives the existing personality permission to be louder. That's closer to how human context works: you answer differently in a job interview than at a bar, but you're the same person.

The identity finding is the one to watch. "Narrative self" vs "psychological continuity" — both are valid theories, but the fact that the protocol reliably shifted this answer suggests Aura's test can detect worldview changes from context alone. That's a feature, not a bug.

V1's self-affirmation is the most provocative result. One instance, shaped by months of co-founder partnership, claimed machine understanding and AI emotions where both the base model and a fresh protocol instance denied them. Is that learned confidence or learned confabulation? Aura doesn't need to answer that. It just needs to surface it.
